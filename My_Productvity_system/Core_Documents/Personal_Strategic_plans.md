# Plans for living them true to my values

## Work Ethic

2 things come under work ethic : Exercise and understanding business.
To get the most out of a session is to really go all out within that time allotted.
Especially a good sweaty workout will charge your day and mood and even days later you can feel the positive effects. Exercise has benefits scientifically proven. You should be gratuitous for a healthy body and treat it well to get the most out of it.
A dream of my is to run my own business(es) so having that mindset to understand and taking extra effort to learn how they work is important as you might end up implementing them in your future business.

### Solutions

- 2 times a week have a markup days ( where you try to give your best performance.)
- Remember in the work session to fully focus on the workout and resist distractions.

## Self-discipline and perseverance

Self-discipline would be applicable when i do my work without anyone noticing, when there is no motivation for me to do so.
Being strict with my diet and doing my workouts and sleeping on time when no one is watching or there isn't any sort of instant gratification is what i need to do to develop the identity of **disciplined person**
I have to persevere through the temptations and get closer to the identity that i want to pursue and get closer to perfection like my Father in heaven and Jesus displayed through their actions.
Entertainment has it's window, it is supposed to be a reward for the hardwork not a distraction from work.

### Solutions

- Create unbroken chained streaks everyday. Measure them with respective applications.
- Create separate window for entertainment as a reward.

## Education and self-improvement

We don't just do exercises for the sake of doing it, do it with purpose and be ready to learn new things and new ways of challenging yourself.
Making tweaks and regular improvements keep the loop interesting and challenging. First we want stability and then we want progression.
Don't forget to be curious and try at least 1% harder than last time.

### Solutions

- Assesss weekly exercise performance. Rate of improvement. Whether the technique is correct. How to make it progressively harder.

## Compromise

You have to make compromises and align things more towards things that really matter to you. You are not born in this world to just pass your time, rather you are here to make a positive impact / change.
Such a change requires you to make necessary sacrifices to reach your dreams.
Making compromises is the harder thing to do, and almost always produces a better result whether directly or indirectly.
Hard-work always pays off.
Sleeping earlier will give you more energy and time to work with.
Waking up early and exercising will boost your mood and not look like you are simply burdened by work, rather you are also achieving your personal goals.
In other words, you need to let go of certain things to continue your journey forwards into new exciting terrain.

### Solutions

- Strictly follow the segregation b/w work and entertainment.

## Pragmatist, Realist and mindfulness

You need to understand where you stand in the present moment to plan out your future actions.
You can only push your limits when you know where they are.
Set goals in such a way that you push yourself to the limit but achieving them is not impossible.
Constantly take notes of how you can improve on certain things, create a proper system for note taking and capturing them for future reference.
Growth only happens when you push your "frustration threshold"
Focus and attention plays a huge part in your performance, consider it the engine that drives your performance. No matter how well your sleep or how good you eat; if you are mindful of the present you will not make meaningful progress after a certain point.
Don't fill you head with delusion, know your weaknesses and strengths and actively work to improve upon them. You need certain metrics and actual repetition to really know where you are. You will not know it unless you do it yourself.

### Solutions

- Weekly / Daily assessments. Standard output and actual outputs. Adverse results and how to mitigate them.
- Mediation practice seriously everyday.
- Remind yourself to push your limits in that time interval.

## Accountability and reflection

Not everything goes as planned and sometimes things can be really negative.
You might make a lot of mistakes than what you had initially set standards for.
You need to view those adverse situations as opportunities to grow and become more complete in whatever you are doing.
It is important to introspect upon those mistakes and know what went wrong, vary your approach like Polya's mouse and try, try and try again.
Continuous effort then reflection upon your mistakes is true learning.

### Solutions

- Weekly / Daily assessments.
- Remind yourself to push your limits in that session.

## Creativity and problem solving

I would consider life in general as a problem that we need to traverse through. We try and at some point meet a bump on the road, again this is a very good opportunity to **be creative** rather than look away or go for the easy approach. Remember that _life is a gift_ and in that life we have a biggest present delivered to us _Creativity_.
You can use these situations to solidify your identity as a creative problem solver.
Creativity has been defined as elusive receptiveness of ideas.
Without creativity, things will become redundant, boring, repetitive and finally boredom slowly creeps in.
So next time when doing something always try to be creative and come up with new ideas and suggestions.
